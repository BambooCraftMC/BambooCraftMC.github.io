Owner Accountability and Leadership Review Policy

This policy is intended to provide a clear and fair process for addressing situations in which an owner believes that another owner may no longer be capable of fulfilling their leadership responsibilities in a healthy, responsible, or effective manner.

If one owner determines that another owner is unwell, inactive, or otherwise unable to properly lead the community, that owner may formally initiate a leadership review. This determination must be made in good faith and for the purpose of protecting the stability and well-being of the community.

Upon initiation of this review, the owner being reviewed will have their roles and ownership permissions temporarily removed. This action is not an immediate judgment or permanent removal, but rather a precautionary measure to ensure that no harm or misuse of authority occurs during the review process.

After roles are removed, the community will be informed and a vote will be held among eligible members. The purpose of this vote is to determine whether the reviewed owner should be reinstated to full ownership or formally demoted from their role.

If the community vote results in demotion, a structured and transparent election process will be conducted to select a new owner. All eligible members will be given the opportunity to participate in the election to ensure fairness and legitimacy.

The outcome of the vote and any resulting ownership changes will be final and enforced in order to preserve trust, accountability, and long-term stability within the community.
